# Trip Planning Principles — China

## TL;DR
- China is vast — don't underestimate distances; pick 2-3 cities max for a 7-day trip
- High-speed rail is the sweet spot for 200-1000km; fly for 1500km+
- Most major attractions require advance online reservation (passport accepted)
- Plan 2-3 attractions per day max; leave buffer for transit and meals
- Museums close on Mondays; check each attraction's closure day before planning

## Scale & Distance

China is roughly the size of the United States or all of Europe combined. Internal distances are much larger than most visitors expect.

### Reference distances

| Route | Distance | Best transport | Travel time |
|-------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Beijing → Shanghai | 1,300 km | High-speed rail | 4.5 hours |
| Beijing → Xi'an | 1,100 km | High-speed rail | 4.5 hours |
| Shanghai → Hangzhou | 180 km | High-speed rail | 1 hour |
| Beijing → Chengdu | 1,800 km | Flight | 3 hours (flight) |
| Shanghai → Guilin | 1,500 km | Flight | 2.5 hours |
| Guangzhou → Hong Kong | 140 km | High-speed rail | 50 min |

### How many cities in N days?

| Trip length | Recommended cities | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| 3-4 days | 1 city | Beijing OR Shanghai |
| 5-7 days | 2 cities | Beijing + Xi'an, or Shanghai + Hangzhou |
| 8-10 days | 2-3 cities | Beijing + Xi'an + Shanghai |
| 12-14 days | 3-4 cities | Beijing + Xi'an + Chengdu + Shanghai |
| 14+ days | 4-5 cities | Add Guilin, Zhangjiajie, Yunnan, etc. |

**Rule of thumb:** Each city move costs half a day (transit to station/airport + travel + check-in). Budget accordingly.

## Transport Selection Rules

### Decision matrix

| Distance | Recommended | Why |
|----------|------------|-----|
| < 200 km | High-speed rail | Faster than flight (no check-in), city-center to city-center |
| 200-1000 km | High-speed rail | Comfortable, frequent, reliable, no security theater |
| 1000-1500 km | Either (preference) | Rail: 4-5 hours, scenic; Flight: 2-3 hours + airport overhead |
| > 1500 km | Flight | Rail takes 7+ hours; flight is significantly faster total |

### High-speed rail advantages for tourists
- Station is in the city center (vs. airports 40-60 min from center)
- No 2-hour early check-in; arrive 30 min before departure
- More frequent (every 15-30 min on major routes)
- Comfortable seats, phone charging, legroom
- No weather delays (rare cancellations)
- Passport booking available on 12306.cn or AliTrip

### When to fly
- Beijing → Chengdu, Beijing → Kunming, Shanghai → Guilin, any route > 1500 km
- When overnight train is the only rail option
- When the price difference is minimal and time matters

## Daily Planning Rules

### Time budget per day

| Block | Hours | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Attractions | 5-7 hours | 2-3 sites at 2-3 hours each |
| Transit between sites | 1-2 hours | Subway + walking |
| Meals | 2 hours | Lunch + dinner (restaurants can be slow) |
| Buffer | 1-2 hours | Getting lost, queues, photo stops, rest |
| Available window | ~10-12 hours | Typical tourist day: 08:00 - 20:00 |

### Attractions per day guideline

| Attraction type | Time needed | Examples |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Major museum / palace | 3-4 hours | Forbidden City, Terracotta Warriors, National Museum |
| Large scenic area | 4-6 hours | Great Wall, West Lake full loop, Jiuzhaigou |
| Temple / park / small museum | 1-2 hours | Temple of Heaven, Lingyin Temple, 798 Art |
| Shopping street / neighborhood | 1.5-2 hours | Nanjing Road, Chengdu Jinli, Xi'an Muslim Quarter |
| Performance / show | 2-3 hours (evening) | Peking Opera, Tang Dynasty show, acrobatics |

**Hard rule:** Never plan more than 1 "major" + 2 "small" attractions in one day. Walking at major sites like the Forbidden City or Great Wall can exceed 20,000 steps.

### Proximity grouping
Always cluster attractions by geographic zone within a city. Do NOT zigzag across the city. Example for Beijing:
- Morning: Forbidden City area (Tiananmen, Forbidden City, Jingshan Park)
- Afternoon: Same zone (Beihai Park) OR adjacent zone (Wangfujing)
- NOT: Forbidden City morning → Summer Palace afternoon → Houhai evening (too spread out)

## Reservation & Booking Rules

### Advance reservation required (CRITICAL)

Many popular attractions in China now require **online reservation days in advance**. You cannot just show up.

| Attraction | Reservation required? | How far ahead | Platform |
|------------|----------------------|---------------|----------|
| Forbidden City (故宫) | **Yes — mandatory** | 7 days ahead | WeChat mini-program or official website |
| Great Wall — Badaling | **Yes** | 1-3 days ahead | WeChat mini-program |
| Great Wall — Mutianyu | **Yes** | 1 day ahead | WeChat mini-program or on-site |
| Terracotta Warriors | **Yes** | 1-3 days ahead | WeChat mini-program |
| National Museum (国博) | **Yes — free but must book** | 1-7 days ahead | WeChat mini-program |
| West Lake (Hangzhou) | No (open area) | — | — |
| The Bund (Shanghai) | No (open area) | — | — |
| Panda Base (Chengdu) | **Yes** | 1-3 days ahead | WeChat mini-program |

**Passport is accepted** for reservation at all major sites — select "护照" (passport) as ID type when booking.

### Reservation tips for foreigners
- Most booking platforms are WeChat mini-programs — have WeChat installed and set up
- Some sites also accept booking via Alipay mini-programs or official English websites
- Book as early as possible during peak seasons (May 1-5, Oct 1-7, summer July-August)
- If fully booked, check again at 8:00 AM — some sites release cancellation slots each morning
- Print or screenshot your QR code — some entry gates have poor signal

## Closure Days & Hours

### Universal rules
- **Most museums close on Mondays** (including Forbidden City, National Museum, provincial museums)
- **Parks and scenic areas** generally open daily (no closure day)
- **Temples** generally open daily
- **Last entry** is typically 30-60 minutes before closing time — don't arrive last minute

### Typical opening hours

| Type | Opens | Last entry | Closes |
|------|-------|-----------|--------|
| Museums | 08:30-09:00 | 15:30-16:00 | 16:30-17:00 |
| Imperial palaces | 08:30 | 15:40 (winter) / 16:10 (summer) | 16:30 / 17:00 |
| Parks | 06:00-06:30 | 20:00-21:00 | 21:00-22:00 |
| Temples | 08:00-08:30 | 16:30-17:00 | 17:00-17:30 |
| Shopping streets | 10:00 | — | 22:00 |

### Seasonal hour variations
- Many attractions have **summer hours** (Apr-Oct, longer) and **winter hours** (Nov-Mar, shorter)
- The Forbidden City closes 30 minutes earlier in winter
- Mountain scenic areas may close trails earlier in winter due to daylight

## Peak Season & Holiday Warnings

### China public holidays (AVOID if possible)

| Holiday | Dates | Impact |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Spring Festival (Chinese New Year)** | Late Jan - mid Feb (varies) | Extreme: transport fully booked 2 weeks ahead, many businesses closed, attractions packed |
| **Qingming (Tomb Sweeping)** | Apr 4-6 | Moderate: short break, some crowding |
| **Labor Day (May Day)** | May 1-5 | Severe: 5-day holiday, all attractions at capacity, prices +50-100% |
| **Dragon Boat Festival** | Early June (varies) | Moderate: 3-day break |
| **Mid-Autumn Festival** | Sept/Oct (varies) | Moderate: 3-day break |
| **National Day (Golden Week)** | Oct 1-7 | Severe: 7-day holiday, worst crowding of the year, prices peak |

### If travel during peak is unavoidable
- Book everything (transport, hotels, attractions) at least 2-3 weeks ahead
- Expect prices 50-100% higher than normal
- Arrive at attractions right at opening time (08:30) to beat crowds
- Consider less-famous alternatives (Mutianyu > Badaling, Huangshan > Great Wall during Golden Week)

## Practical Constraints for Foreigners

### Passport as ID
- China's system is designed around national ID cards (身份证) — but **passport works** at all tourist-facing systems
- When booking anything online: select "护照" (passport) as document type
- Carry your passport at all times — hotels, trains, attractions, and even some subway security checks may ask for it

### Hotel registration
- Chinese law requires hotels to register foreign guests with local police within 24 hours
- Stick to licensed hotels (not unregistered homestays) — they know how to handle foreign passports
- Budget hostels and international chains both work fine
- If staying with friends, your host must register you at the local police station within 24 hours

### Communication
- Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and many Western apps are blocked in China
- Download a VPN before arrival if you need these (but turn VPN off when using Alipay/WeChat Pay)
- WeChat is the universal communication tool — hotels, guides, and locals all use it
- Amap (高德地图) is the best navigation app in China; Google Maps works poorly

### Language
- English is limited outside international hotels and major tourist zones
- Translation apps (Google Translate with offline Chinese pack, or Apple Translate) are essential
- Learn 3 phrases: 你好 (nǐ hǎo = hello), 谢谢 (xièxie = thanks), 这个 (zhège = this one — for pointing at menus)
- Many restaurants have picture menus or QR code ordering (scan with WeChat)

## Itinerary Output Rules (for AI assistant)

When generating itineraries, follow these constraints:

1. **Always validate against the rules above** — don't place 4 major attractions in one day
2. **Show transit time explicitly** — "Metro Line 1, 25 min" between attractions
3. **Flag reservation requirements** — mark any attraction that needs advance booking with a note
4. **Check closure day** — don't schedule a museum on Monday
5. **Include a morning/afternoon/evening rhythm** — not just a flat list
6. **Add meal slots** — tourists need to eat; suggest general areas, not specific restaurants
7. **Provide booking guidance** — end with "How to book" linking to Alipay AliTrip or specific reservation platforms
8. **Note peak season impact** — if user's dates fall on/near a holiday, warn proactively
