# China Visa — What Foreign Tourists Need to Know

## TL;DR
- **Many countries now enjoy visa-free entry** — 30-day stay, no visa needed (expanded significantly since late 2023)
- **240-hour visa-free transit** — available at major cities for 55 nationalities, no visa needed if transiting to a third country
- **Regular tourist visa (L visa)** — apply at a Chinese embassy/consulate if neither option above applies
- **Check your nationality first** — the right entry method depends entirely on your passport
- Policies change frequently — verify current status before booking

## Entry Methods at a Glance

| Method | Who qualifies | Max stay | Effort | Best for |
|--------|--------------|----------|--------|----------|
| **Visa-free entry** (mutual exemption) | 29 countries | 15–30 days | None | Short trips — tourism, business, family visit |
| **Visa-free entry** (unilateral) | 50 countries* | 15–30 days | None | Short trips — tourism, business, family visit |
| **240-hour visa-free transit** | 55 countries | 10 days (240h) | Low (just need onward ticket) | Stopovers, short city visits en route |
| **24-hour visa-free transit** | All nationalities | 24 hours | None (stay in port area) | Very short layovers, no visa needed |
| **Hainan 30-day visa-free** | 59 countries | 30 days | Low (group or individual via travel agency) | Beach holiday in Hainan only |
| **Tourist visa (L visa)** | All nationalities | 30–90 days | Medium (embassy application) | Longer trips, nationalities without visa-free |

*The 50 unilateral visa-free countries include 4 that also have mutual exemption agreements (Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, Brunei). They appear in both categories but are counted once in the unilateral list for simplicity. The total number of unique visa-free countries is approximately 75.

## Which Option Is Right for Me?

### Step 1: Check if your country has visa-free entry
See `visa-free-countries.md` for the full list. If yes → you can enter China directly for 15–30 days with just your passport. No application needed.

### Step 2: If not visa-free, check 240-hour transit
If you're passing through China to a third country → you likely qualify for 240-hour visa-free transit at major cities. See `visa-free-transit.md` for details.

### Step 3: Otherwise, apply for a tourist visa
Apply at your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate. See the "Tourist Visa (L Visa)" section below.

## Visa-Free Entry

Since late 2023, China has rapidly expanded visa-free agreements. Citizens of eligible countries can enter China **without any visa** for tourism, business, family visits, or transit.

There are two categories:
- **Mutual exemption** (29 countries): bilateral agreements where both sides grant visa-free access (e.g., Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, Serbia, UAE)
- **Unilateral visa-free** (50 countries): China unilaterally grants visa-free entry, often as trial policies renewed annually (e.g., France, Germany, UK, Canada, Australia, South Korea, Japan)

**Key facts:**
- Stay duration: typically 15 or 30 days depending on agreement
- Entry purpose: tourism, business, visiting relatives, transit
- No pre-registration or application needed — just show your passport at immigration
- Can travel anywhere in China (no city restrictions)
- Can enter/exit from any port (airport, land border, sea port)

**Common eligible countries include:** France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Switzerland, Ireland, Hungary, Austria, Belgium, Luxembourg, Poland, Slovakia, Czech Republic, Greece, Portugal, Finland, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Australia, New Zealand, Canada, United Kingdom, Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, South Korea, Japan, and more.

For the complete and current list → see `visa-free-countries.md`

**Important:** Unilateral visa-free policies are often announced as "trial periods" (current deadline: December 31, 2026) and get extended. Always verify the current status before travel.

## 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit

A powerful option if you're flying through China to another country. Stay up to 10 days — no visa needed, with cross-provincial travel allowed.

**Key facts:**
- Available to citizens of 55 countries
- Must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third country/region (not your origin country)
- Covers **24 provinces**: 18 are full-province scope (travel anywhere in the province), 6 are city-restricted
- **Cross-provincial travel is allowed** within the permitted areas — you can enter through one port and exit from another in a different province
- Available at 65 ports across 24 provinces (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Xi'an, Hangzhou, etc.)
- 7 provinces are NOT covered: Inner Mongolia, Jilin, Tibet, Gansu, Qinghai, Ningxia, Xinjiang

For full details, eligible countries, ports, and rules → see `visa-free-transit.md`

## 24-Hour Visa-Free Transit

Available to **all nationalities** at **all internationally open ports** (airports, sea ports, and land ports). The most basic transit option — no visa needed if you stay within the port area.

**Key facts:**
- Available to every nationality worldwide
- Must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third country/region departing within 24 hours
- If you stay **within the port area**: no visa or permit needed
- If you need to **leave the port area** (e.g., to stay at a hotel): you must apply for a temporary entry permit (临时入境许可) at the port's immigration authority
- The 24-hour clock starts from the time of arrival

**Best for:** Very short layovers where you don't need to leave the airport, or emergencies where you need to enter briefly before continuing your journey.

**Note:** If your country is on the 240-hour eligible list and your port supports it, the 240-hour option is almost always better — it gives you 10 days and freedom to travel within the designated area.

For more details → see `visa-free-transit.md`

## Tourist Visa (L Visa)

If you don't qualify for visa-free options, apply for an L (tourist) visa at a Chinese embassy or consulate.

### Application requirements
1. **Passport** — valid for 6+ months with at least 2 blank pages
2. **Application form** — download from embassy website or use the COVA online system
3. **Photo** — recent passport-style photo (white background, 48mm × 33mm)
4. **Round-trip flight booking** — or itinerary showing entry and exit
5. **Hotel reservation** — for at least the first few nights
6. **Proof of financial means** — bank statement (varies by embassy)
7. **Invitation letter** — sometimes required, depends on embassy

### Process
1. Find your nearest Chinese embassy/consulate (search: "Chinese embassy in [your country]")
2. Many embassies now use the **COVA** online system — fill out the application online, then submit documents in person or via visa center
3. Processing time: typically 4–7 business days (express available at some locations)
4. Cost: varies by nationality and entry type (single/double/multiple)
5. Pick up your passport with visa stamped inside

### Visa types you might get
| Type | Entries | Validity | Stay per visit |
|------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Single entry | 1 | 3 months | 30 days |
| Double entry | 2 | 6 months | 30 days each |
| Multiple entry | Unlimited | 6–12 months | 60–90 days each |

**Note:** "Validity" is how long you have to use the visa; "stay" is how long you can remain each visit. They are different.

### Tips for smooth application
- Apply 4–6 weeks before travel (not too early, not too late)
- Visa centers (like CVASC) handle applications for many embassies — check if your country uses one
- Some nationalities face longer processing — start early
- Group tours may have simplified requirements
- If denied, you can reapply — address the reason for denial

## Hainan 30-Day Visa-Free

Citizens of **59 countries** can visit **Hainan Island only** for up to 30 days without a visa. This is a separate policy from the national-level visa-free entry — some countries qualify for Hainan but NOT for national visa-free entry (e.g., Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam).

### Eligible countries (59)

**Europe (40):** Austria, Belgium, Belarus, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Russia, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Albania, Bulgaria, Ukraine

**Asia (11):** Indonesia, Japan, Kazakhstan, South Korea, Laos, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Brunei, Thailand, Vietnam

**Americas (6):** Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Mexico, United States

**Oceania (2):** Australia, New Zealand

**Note:** Many of these countries also qualify for national-level visa-free entry (which covers all of China, not just Hainan). The Hainan policy is mainly useful for nationals of countries like Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam, Laos, Ukraine, Mexico, and the United States that are on the Hainan list but NOT on the national unilateral visa-free list. Combined with national-level visa-free and mutual exemption policies, Hainan's total visa-free coverage is significantly broader than the 59-country base.

### Conditions
- Must book through a registered travel agency in Hainan (even for individual travel)
- Entry and exit must be through Hainan ports (Haikou or Sanya airports)
- Cannot leave Hainan Island during the stay
- Available for tourism only

**Best for:** Beach holidays, resort stays on Hainan. Not suitable for multi-city China trips.

## Regional Visa-Free Policies

Beyond the national-level and Hainan policies, China offers several region-specific visa-free entry options. These are narrower in scope but useful for specific itineraries.

### Pearl River Delta / Guangdong 144-hour visa-free

Foreign nationals from countries with diplomatic relations with China can visit the Pearl River Delta region for up to **144 hours (6 days)** without a visa, subject to these conditions:

- Must enter China **via Hong Kong or Macau** as part of an organized group tour (minimum 2 people) arranged by a registered travel agency
- Stay is restricted to **10 cities**: Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Zhuhai, Foshan, Dongguan, Zhongshan, Jiangmen, Zhaoqing, Huizhou, and Shantou
- Must enter and exit as a group through designated ports

**Best for:** Short group tours from Hong Kong or Macau into the Greater Bay Area.

### Guilin ASEAN 144-hour visa-free

Citizens of the **10 ASEAN member states** (Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam) can visit Guilin for up to **144 hours (6 days)** without a visa:

- Must enter through **Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL)**
- Must travel as part of an organized group tour arranged by a registered travel agency
- Stay restricted to the **Guilin area**

**Best for:** Short group tours to see Guilin's famous karst landscape and Li River.

### Xishuangbanna ASEAN 6-day visa-free

Since February 2025, citizens of the **10 ASEAN member states** can visit Xishuangbanna (Yunnan Province) for up to **6 days** without a visa:

- Must enter through designated **land border ports** in Xishuangbanna
- Must travel as part of an organized group tour
- Stay restricted to **Xishuangbanna Prefecture**

**Best for:** Southeast Asian tourists visiting the tropical border region of Yunnan.

### Cruise ship visa-free (15 days)

Foreign tourists arriving by **cruise ship** at any of China's **13 coastal cruise ports** can stay for up to **15 days** visa-free:

- Available to **all nationalities**
- Must be part of an organized tour group arranged by the cruise company or a registered Chinese travel agency
- Stay is limited to the port city and designated surrounding areas
- Ports include: Shanghai, Tianjin, Guangzhou, Xiamen, Dalian, Qingdao, Sanya, Haikou, Shenzhen, Beihai, Yantai, Wenzhou, Zhoushan

**Best for:** Cruise passengers on multi-day Asian itineraries with China port stops.

## At the Border — What to Expect

### Documents to have ready
- Passport (valid 6+ months)
- Visa or proof of visa-free eligibility
- For 240h transit: onward ticket confirmation (printed or on phone)
- Arrival card (distributed on plane or available at immigration counters)
- Hotel address in China

### Arrival card
You'll fill out a brief arrival/departure card. Information needed:
- Full name, nationality, passport number
- Flight number
- Purpose of visit
- Address in China (hotel name and city is sufficient)

**Online option (since November 2025):** You can fill out the arrival card digitally before departure using:
- NIA website (s.nia.gov.cn)
- "移民局12367" App
- WeChat or Alipay mini-programs
- QR code (available at ports)

Online submission is **optional** — paper forms are still available at immigration counters. Exemptions include permanent residents, 24-hour transit passengers, cruise round-trippers, and fast-track users.

### Fingerprints
China collects fingerprints from foreign visitors aged 14–70 at immigration. This is routine and takes about 30 seconds.

**Consular exemption (since December 2025):** Short-term visa applicants (stay ≤180 days, any entry type — single, double, or multiple) are exempt from fingerprint collection at Chinese consulates/embassies during the visa application process. Previously, this exemption was limited to single- and double-entry applicants only. Note: this is about the **consular application process**, not the border entry fingerprinting described above — you will still be fingerprinted at immigration upon arrival in China.

### Customs declaration
If carrying more than $5,000 USD (or equivalent) in cash, or restricted items (food, plants, animals), fill out a customs declaration form.

## FAQ

### Do I need a visa if my country has visa-free entry?
No. Just bring your valid passport. Check `visa-free-countries.md` to confirm your country is on the list and verify the current status.

### Can I extend my stay?
Yes. Visit the local Public Security Bureau (PSB) Exit-Entry Administration before your permitted stay expires. Extensions are not guaranteed but commonly granted for 30 additional days for tourists.

### What if I overstay?
Overstaying is taken seriously: fines of ¥500/day (capped at ¥10,000), possible detention, and entry ban. Always leave before your permitted stay expires or apply for extension.

### Is Hong Kong/Macau the same as mainland China?
No. Hong Kong and Macau have separate immigration systems. Entering Hong Kong or Macau from mainland China counts as "exiting China." If on 240h transit, your transit ends when you enter HK/Macau. However, HK/Macau can be your "third country" destination for 240h transit purposes.

### Can I enter China from Hong Kong on 240h transit?
Yes — some ports support this. The "third country" can be Hong Kong or Macau. Check `visa-free-transit.md` for port details.

### Do I need a return ticket?
- Visa-free entry: not required, but immigration may ask about your travel plan
- 240h transit: **yes**, you must have a confirmed ticket to a third country
- L visa: a return or onward booking is typically required for the visa application

### Can I work in China on a tourist visa or visa-free entry?
No. Tourism entry does not permit work. You need a separate work visa (Z visa) for employment.

## Gotchas
- **Visa-free policies change frequently** — always verify current status 1–2 weeks before travel on the Chinese embassy website for your country
- **240h transit ≠ visa-free entry** — transit visa-free has city restrictions and requires an onward ticket; visa-free entry has neither
- **Passport validity matters** — some airlines and immigration require 6+ months remaining; don't travel on a nearly-expired passport
- **Tibet requires a special permit** — even with a valid visa or visa-free entry, foreigners need a Tibet Travel Permit arranged through a registered agency
- **Some land borders have different rules** — visa-free transit is mainly designed for air travelers; check specific ports if entering by land
- **"Third country" cannot be your origin** — for 240h transit, your onward destination must be different from where you flew in from
