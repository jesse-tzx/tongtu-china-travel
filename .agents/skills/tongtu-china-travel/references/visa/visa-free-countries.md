# Visa-Free Entry to China — Country List

## TL;DR
- China has granted **visa-free entry** to citizens of 50 countries (expanded rapidly since Nov 2023)
- Most agreements allow **15 or 30 days** stay for tourism, business, family visits, and transit
- **No application, no fee, no pre-registration** — just show your passport at immigration
- You can travel **anywhere in China** (no city restrictions, unlike 240h transit)
- Policies are often "trial periods" that get renewed — always verify current status before travel

## How Visa-Free Entry Works

### What it means
If your country is on the list below, you can enter China **without applying for a visa**. Simply:
1. Fly to China (or arrive by land/sea)
2. Present your passport at immigration
3. Get an entry stamp
4. Travel freely anywhere in China for up to the permitted number of days

### Key differences from 240h transit visa-free

| | Visa-free entry | 240h transit visa-free |
|---|----------------|----------------------|
| Need onward ticket to third country? | **No** | Yes (mandatory) |
| City/area restriction? | **None** — travel anywhere | Yes — stay within permitted area (18 full-province, 6 city-restricted; cross-provincial travel allowed) |
| Max stay | 15 or 30 days | 10 days (240 hours) |
| Can enter and exit same country? | **Yes** | No (third country rule) |
| Number of eligible countries | 50 | 55 |

**Bottom line:** If your country has visa-free entry, that's always the better option. Use 240h transit only if your country doesn't qualify for full visa-free.

## Countries NOT on the Visa-Free List

### Major tourist-origin countries that still need a visa
- **United States** — not visa-free (use 240h transit or apply for L visa)
- **India** — not visa-free, not eligible for 240h transit either (apply for L visa)
- **Philippines** — not visa-free (apply for L visa)
- **Vietnam** — not visa-free (apply for L visa)
- **Indonesia** — not visa-free (apply for L visa)

**For these countries:** Your best option is either the 240-hour visa-free transit (if you're passing through China) or a regular L visa (if China is your primary destination). See `overview.md` for details.

## Rules & Conditions

### Permitted activities
- Tourism and sightseeing
- Business meetings (not employment)
- Visiting relatives and friends
- Transit
- Short-term private activities

### NOT permitted
- Work or employment (need Z visa)
- Study longer than short-term visits (need X visa)
- Journalism/media reporting (need J visa)

### Entry requirements
1. **Valid passport** — must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned stay
2. **No other restrictions** — no prior entry bans, deportation history, or national security flags
3. **Purpose within scope** — tourism, business, family visit, or transit

### Can I extend my stay?
Yes, in some cases. Visit the local PSB (Public Security Bureau) Exit-Entry Administration office **before** your permitted stay expires. You may be granted a 30-day extension. This is not guaranteed — apply at least 7 days before expiry.

### Multiple entries
Most visa-free agreements do not limit the number of entries per year. However:
- Each entry restarts the clock (e.g., 30 days per visit, not cumulative)
- Immigration may question frequent short visits — have a reasonable explanation
- If you need to stay longer than 30 days total, consider a multi-entry visa instead

## "Trial Policy" — What It Means

Many of the newer visa-free agreements (especially European and Oceanian countries) are announced as "trial policies" with end dates. In practice:
- China has consistently **extended** these trial periods when they expire
- The direction of travel is toward more openness, not less
- However, policies **can** be suspended with short notice (Japan was suspended 2020–2024)

**Recommendation:** Check the Chinese embassy website for your country **1–2 weeks before departure** to confirm the policy is still active. A quick check takes 2 minutes and prevents a very bad surprise at the airport.

## FAQ

### How do I know if the policy is still active?
Check the Chinese embassy/consulate website for your country. Look for "visa exemption" or "visa-free" announcements. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs website (fmprc.gov.cn) also publishes updates.

### Do I need to show a return ticket?
Not technically required for visa-free entry (unlike 240h transit). However, airlines may require a return/onward ticket before boarding, and immigration officers may ask about your travel plans. Having a booking (even refundable) is recommended.

### Can I go to Tibet?
Technically yes (visa-free gives you access to all of China), but Tibet still requires a **Tibet Travel Permit** arranged through a registered travel agency at least 2–3 weeks in advance. The permit is separate from visa/entry rights.

### What happens at day 30 (or 15)?
You must leave China by 24:00 on your last permitted day. The day of arrival counts as day 1.

### I'm a dual citizen — which passport do I use?
Use the passport of the country that has visa-free access. Immigration only sees the passport you present. Note: China does not recognize dual citizenship for Chinese nationals.

### My country just got added to the list — is it immediate?
Each announcement specifies an effective date. You cannot use the policy before that date.

### Can I fill out the arrival card online?
Yes. Since November 2025, you can fill out the arrival card digitally before departure via the NIA website (s.nia.gov.cn), "移民局12367" App, or WeChat/Alipay mini-programs. This is optional — paper forms are still available at immigration counters.

## Gotchas
- **"Trial policy" dates** — these visa-free agreements have expiry dates and may not be renewed (unlikely but possible). Always verify before travel
- **Airlines may not know** — some airline ground staff may be unaware of China's latest visa-free policies and try to deny boarding. Carry a printout of the official announcement from the Chinese embassy as backup
- **Overstay is serious** — ¥500/day fine, possible detention and entry ban. Set a phone reminder for 3 days before your departure deadline
- **Day counting** — arrival day = day 1. If you arrive Jan 1, your 30 days end Jan 30 (not Jan 31)
- **Hong Kong ≠ mainland China** — visa-free entry to China does not cover Hong Kong or Macau (they have their own policies, which are generally more open). Entering HK/Macau from mainland counts as exiting China
- **COVID-era policies are fully lifted** — as of 2023, all COVID-related entry restrictions (testing, quarantine, health codes) have been removed. Normal pre-2020 entry procedures apply
