# 240-Hour / 24-Hour Visa-Free Transit

## TL;DR
- **240-hour transit**: Citizens of **55 countries** can transit through China for up to **10 days (240 hours)** without a visa
- **24-hour transit**: Available to **all nationalities** at **all open ports** — stay within the port area, no visa needed
- For 240h transit: You MUST have a confirmed onward ticket to a **third country** (not your origin country)
- For 240h transit: You can only stay within the **designated area** of your entry port (usually the city/province)
- Available at **65 ports** across 24 provinces — all major cities covered (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Xi'an, Hangzhou, etc.)
- Free, no application needed — just declare at immigration upon arrival

## Who Qualifies

### Eligible nationalities (55 countries)

**Europe (40):**
Albania, Austria, Belarus, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Ukraine, United Kingdom, Russia

**Americas (6):**
Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Mexico, United States

**Oceania (2):**
Australia, New Zealand

**Asia (7):**
Brunei, Japan, Qatar, Singapore, South Korea, United Arab Emirates, Indonesia

### Conditions (ALL must be met)

1. **Valid passport** from one of the 55 eligible countries
2. **Confirmed onward ticket** departing China to a third country/region within 240 hours
3. **Third country rule**: Your onward destination must be different from your origin country (the country you flew in from)
4. **Enter and exit through designated ports** (see port list below)
5. **Stay within the permitted area** — 18 provinces are full-province scope, 6 are city-restricted, and cross-provincial travel is allowed within the 24 provinces (see area restrictions)

## How It Works

### Step-by-step at the airport

1. **On the plane**: When filling out the arrival card, write "240-hour visa-free transit" as purpose of visit. You can also fill out the card digitally before departure via the NIA website (s.nia.gov.cn), "移民局12367" App, or WeChat/Alipay mini-programs (optional — paper forms still available)
2. **At immigration**: Go to the regular foreigners' queue (not the visa-free/Chinese citizens lane)
3. **Tell the officer**: "I'm applying for 240-hour visa-free transit"
4. **Show these documents**:
   - Your passport
   - Boarding pass or booking confirmation for your onward flight (to a third country)
   - Your accommodation details in China (hotel name + address)
5. **Get stamped**: The officer stamps a temporary entry permit in your passport — it shows your allowed stay area and deadline
6. **Done** — proceed to baggage claim and customs

### No pre-application needed
Unlike a visa, there is no form to fill out in advance, no fee, and no waiting period. Everything happens at the immigration counter upon arrival.

### 24-hour transit (all nationalities)
China's 24-hour visa-free transit applies to **all nationalities** at **all internationally open ports** (airports, sea ports, and land ports).

**Key rules:**
- You must hold a valid passport and a confirmed onward ticket to a third country/region departing within 24 hours
- If you **stay within the port area** and do not pass through immigration: no visa or permit needed
- If you need to **leave the port area** (e.g., to stay at a hotel outside the terminal): you must apply for a **temporary entry permit** (临时入境许可) at the port's immigration inspection authority
- The 24-hour clock starts from the time of arrival

**Best for:** Very short layovers where you don't need to leave the airport, or emergencies where you need to enter briefly before continuing your journey.

**Note:** The 24-hour policy is separate from the 240-hour policy. If your country is on the 240-hour eligible list and your port supports it, the 240-hour option is almost always better — it gives you 10 days and freedom to travel within the designated area.

## Area Restrictions — What You Can and Cannot Do

### The 24-province coverage

The 240-hour transit policy covers **24 provinces** (autonomous regions, municipalities), and importantly, **cross-provincial travel is allowed** within the permitted areas. This is a major upgrade from the old 144-hour policy.

**Full-province scope (18 provinces):**
You can travel anywhere within the entire province:

Beijing, Tianjin, Hebei, Liaoning, Shanghai, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Anhui, Fujian, Shandong, Henan, Hubei, Hunan, Guangdong, Hainan, Chongqing, Guizhou, Shaanxi

**City-restricted scope (6 provinces):**
You can only visit designated cities within these provinces:

- **Shanxi**: Taiyuan, Datong
- **Heilongjiang**: Harbin
- **Jiangxi**: Nanchang, Jingdezhen
- **Guangxi**: Nanning, Liuzhou, Guilin, Wuzhou, Beihai, Fangchenggang, Qinzhou, Guigang, Yulin, Hezhou, Hechi, Laibin (12 cities)
- **Sichuan**: Chengdu, Zigong, Luzhou, Deyang, Suining, Neijiang, Leshan, Yibin, Ya'an, Meishan, Ziyang (11 cities)
- **Yunnan**: Kunming and 8 other designated cities/prefectures

**Not covered (7 provinces/regions):**
Inner Mongolia, Jilin, Tibet, Gansu, Qinghai, Ningxia, Xinjiang — the 240-hour transit policy does not apply to these areas.

### Cross-provincial travel

You **can** cross provincial boundaries within the permitted areas of the 24 provinces. For example:
- Enter through Guangzhou (Guangdong, full province) → travel to Hunan (full province) → exit from Changsha to a third country → **OK**
- Enter through Beijing → visit Tianjin and Hebei → travel to Shandong → exit from Qingdao → **OK**
- Enter through Shanghai → visit Jiangsu and Zhejiang → **OK** (all three are full-province scope)

**Guangdong special rule:** Guangdong allows exit from any open port across the province, making it especially flexible for multi-city itineraries.

### Practical implications

| I entered at... | I CAN visit... | I CANNOT visit... |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Beijing (PEK/PKX) | Beijing, Great Wall, Tianjin, Chengde, plus cross-province to Shandong, Hebei, etc. | Tibet, Inner Mongolia, Xinjiang (excluded provinces) |
| Shanghai (PVG/SHA) | Shanghai, Hangzhou, Suzhou, Nanjing, West Lake, plus cross-province to Anhui, Fujian, etc. | Tibet, Gansu, Qinghai (excluded provinces) |
| Guangzhou (CAN) | All of Guangdong (Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Zhuhai, etc.), plus cross-province to Hunan, Guangxi, etc. | Tibet, Inner Mongolia, Xinjiang (excluded provinces) |
| Chengdu (TFU/CTU) | Chengdu, Leshan Giant Buddha, and 9 other designated Sichuan cities | Jiuzhaigou (Aba Prefecture, not in designated list), excluded provinces |
| Xi'an (XIY) | All of Shaanxi province (Xi'an, Terracotta Warriors, Xianyang, Huashan, etc.), plus cross-province | Tibet, Gansu, Qinghai (excluded provinces) |

### What if I want to visit multiple cities across different provinces?
- If both provinces are in the **full-province scope** list (e.g., Shanghai + Zhejiang + Jiangsu) → OK, travel freely
- If one province is **city-restricted** (e.g., Sichuan) → you can only visit the designated cities within that province
- To visit **excluded provinces** (Tibet, Inner Mongolia, etc.) → you need either a **visa-free entry** (if your country qualifies) or a **regular visa**
- You can also do separate 240h transits on different trips

## Counting the 240 Hours

### When does the clock start?
From **00:00 on the day after arrival**. Example:
- Arrive Monday at 22:00 → clock starts Tuesday 00:00 → must depart before the following Thursday 24:00
- This gives you a generous window — effectively 10 full days

### When must I leave?
Before your 240 hours expire. The exact deadline is stamped in your passport by the immigration officer.

### What if my onward flight is delayed?
If your departure is delayed due to force majeure (weather, airline cancellation), report to the local PSB immediately. They can extend your stay — but you must report proactively, not after the deadline.

## FAQ

### Does my onward ticket need to be a flight?
No. It can be a flight, train, bus, or ferry — as long as it leaves China to a third country/region. However, a flight is the most commonly accepted proof.

### Can I exit from a different port than I entered?
Yes, as long as both ports are within the same designated area. Example: enter at Shanghai Pudong (PVG), exit at Hangzhou (HGH) — both are in the Shanghai-Jiangsu-Zhejiang zone.

### Can my "third country" be Hong Kong or Macau?
Yes. Hong Kong and Macau count as separate destinations for this policy. A common route: fly into Shanghai from Seoul, transit 240h, fly out to Hong Kong.

### Can I come back for another 240h transit later?
Yes. There's no limit on how many times you use this policy. Each transit is independent.

### What if I miss my onward flight?
Rebook immediately. If you cannot depart before the 240h deadline, go to the local PSB to apply for a regular visa or extension before your time runs out. Do not overstay.

### Can I enter by land/sea?
Some ports include land and sea crossings (e.g., Shenzhen has land ports to Hong Kong). Check the specific port list. Most tourists use airports.

### Do I need a hotel booking?
Immigration may ask for your accommodation address. Have at least your first night's hotel name and address ready (printed or on your phone).

### Can I take a domestic flight within the designated area?
Yes, as long as you stay within the zone. Example: in the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei zone, you could technically fly between airports within the zone. In practice, trains are more common.

## Gotchas
- **"Third country" rule is strict** — flying Tokyo → Beijing → Tokyo does NOT qualify (same origin and destination). You need Tokyo → Beijing → Seoul (or any different country)
- **Don't leave your zone** — if caught outside the designated area, you face fines and potential detention. GPS on your phone doesn't enforce this, but random checks at hotels and train stations do (hotels register foreign guests with police)
- **Stamp carefully** — the immigration officer writes your deadline by hand. Double-check it's correct and legible
- **Hotels must register you** — Chinese law requires hotels to register foreign guests with local police within 24 hours. Stick to regular hotels (not unregistered Airbnbs) to avoid issues
- **Re-entry resets nothing** — if you leave China and re-enter, you start a fresh 240h period (assuming you meet all conditions again)
- **Tibet is not accessible** — even within your designated area, Tibet requires a separate permit that takes weeks to arrange. Not compatible with 240h transit
